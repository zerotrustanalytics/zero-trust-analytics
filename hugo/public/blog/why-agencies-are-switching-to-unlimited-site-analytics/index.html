Why Agencies Are Switching to Unlimited-Site Analytics

Per-site pricing is crushing agency margins. Here's why unlimited-site analytics plans are the smart choice for marketing agencies.

February 17, 2025 4 min read Zero Trust Analytics Team

You manage 50 client websites. Each one needs analytics. Traditional pricing models make this expensive:

  • Google Analytics is “free” but creates compliance nightmares
  • Plausible charges per site ($9 × 50 = $450/month)
  • Fathom charges per site with pageview limits
  • Most tools get expensive quickly at scale

There has to be a better way.

The Agency Analytics Problem

Marketing agencies have unique analytics needs:

Scale

  • Dozens or hundreds of client sites
  • New clients onboarding constantly
  • Varying traffic levels across sites
  • Need consistent reporting

Client Expectations

  • Clients want privacy-friendly options
  • GDPR/CCPA questions come up in sales calls
  • “What data are you collecting?” is a common concern
  • Cookie banners frustrate clients

Margin Pressure

  • Clients expect analytics as part of the package
  • Per-site costs eat into margins
  • Can’t easily pass costs through
  • Need predictable expenses

Traditional per-site pricing doesn’t work for this model.

Why Per-Site Pricing Fails Agencies

The Math Problem

Let’s say you manage 50 client sites:

Service Per-Site Cost Total Monthly
Plausible $9 × 50 $450
Fathom ~$15 × 50 $750
Simple Analytics $19 × 50 $950

That’s significant overhead that either:

  • Eats your margin
  • Gets passed to clients (reducing competitiveness)
  • Forces you to use “free” options with compliance problems

The Scaling Problem

Every new client increases costs. This creates friction in sales:

  • “Should we include analytics in this proposal?”
  • “Can we absorb another $9-19/month?”
  • “What if the client churns in 3 months?”

Analytics becomes a cost center instead of a value-add.

The Management Problem

50 separate accounts means:

  • 50 logins to manage
  • 50 separate bills
  • No consolidated view
  • Onboarding overhead for each site

Administrative nightmare.

The Unlimited-Site Advantage

The Scaling (Solved)

New client? Add them to your existing account. No incremental cost. No margin calculation. No friction in the sales process.

“Yes, we include privacy-first analytics” becomes an easy answer.

The Management (Simplified)

This is how agency tools should work.

Beyond Pricing: The Compliance Advantage

Unlimited sites is just the beginning. For agencies, our privacy-first approach solves bigger problems:

GDPR Questions in Sales

Client asks: “What about GDPR compliance?”

Your answer: “We use privacy-first analytics that don’t require consent. No cookies, no personal data collection, no compliance concerns.”

Compliance becomes a selling point instead of a liability.

Cookie Banner Discussions

Client asks: “Do we need a cookie consent banner?”

Your answer: “Not for analytics. Our tool doesn’t use cookies.”

Simpler websites. Better UX. Easier client conversations.

Data Processing Agreements

Client asks: “Do we need a DPA for analytics?”

Your answer: “Our analytics don’t process personal data, so no DPA is needed.”

Less paperwork. Faster onboarding.

White-Label Options

For agencies on our Scale plan, we offer white-label options:

  • Custom domain for analytics dashboard
  • Your branding on reports
  • Client-facing access without our branding
  • API access for custom integrations

Your clients see your agency’s analytics solution, not a third-party tool.

Real Agency Scenarios

Scenario 1: The Web Design Agency

You build websites for small businesses. Each client needs analytics. Previously:

  • Helped clients set up Google Analytics (compliance risk transferred)
  • Or ate the cost of per-site tools
  • Or skipped analytics entirely

Now: Include privacy-first analytics in every project. One subscription covers all clients. Better service, better margin.

Scenario 2: The Digital Marketing Agency

You run campaigns across 100+ client sites. Need to measure performance. Previously:

  • Complex Google Analytics setups
  • Cookie consent on every landing page
  • 30%+ traffic invisible to ad blockers

Now: Accurate analytics without ad blocker losses. No consent barriers. Clear campaign attribution.

Scenario 3: The Privacy-Focused Agency

Your agency specializes in privacy-conscious clients. They expect you to practice what you preach. Previously:

  • Awkward conversations about tracking tools
  • Cobbled-together solutions
  • Inconsistent approach

Now: Privacy-first analytics that align with your values. Easy to explain. Easy to defend.

Making the Switch

Moving an agency to Zero Trust Analytics:

Week 1:

  1. Sign up for Pro or Business plan
  2. Add your top 10 client sites
  3. Verify data flows correctly
  4. Get comfortable with the dashboard

Week 2-3:

Agencies using privacy-first analytics gain advantages:

  • Sales differentiation: “We don’t surveil your customers”
  • Faster onboarding: No consent setup, no DPAs
  • Better data: No ad blocker losses
  • Simpler operations: One tool, all clients
  • Protected margins: Fixed cost regardless of growth

Privacy-first isn’t just an ethical position—it’s a business strategy.
