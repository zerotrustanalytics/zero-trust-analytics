You manage 50 client websites. Each one needs analytics. Traditional pricing models make this expensive:

- Google Analytics is "free" but creates compliance nightmares
- Plausible charges per site ($9 × 50 = $450/month)
- Fathom charges per site with pageview limits
- Most tools get expensive quickly at scale

**There has to be a better way.**

## The Agency Analytics Problem

Marketing agencies have unique analytics needs:

### Scale
- Dozens or hundreds of client sites
- New clients onboarding constantly
- Varying traffic levels across sites
- Need consistent reporting

### Client Expectations
- Clients want privacy-friendly options
- GDPR/CCPA questions come up in sales calls
- "What data are you collecting?" is a common concern
- Cookie banners frustrate clients

### Margin Pressure
- Clients expect analytics as part of the package
- Per-site costs eat into margins
- Can't easily pass costs through
- Need predictable expenses

Traditional per-site pricing doesn't work for this model.

## Why Per-Site Pricing Fails Agencies

### The Math Problem

Let's say you manage 50 client sites:

| Service | Per-Site Cost | Total Monthly |
|---------|--------------|---------------|
| Plausible | $9 × 50 | $450 |
| Fathom | ~$15 × 50 | $750 |
| Simple Analytics | $19 × 50 | $950 |

That's significant overhead that either:
- Eats your margin
- Gets passed to clients (reducing competitiveness)
- Forces you to use "free" options with compliance problems

### The Scaling Problem

Every new client increases costs. This creates friction in sales:
- "Should we include analytics in this proposal?"
- "Can we absorb another $9-19/month?"
- "What if the client churns in 3 months?"

Analytics becomes a cost center instead of a value-add.

### The Management Problem

50 separate accounts means:
- 50 logins to manage
- 50 separate bills
- No consolidated view
- Onboarding overhead for each site

Administrative nightmare.
